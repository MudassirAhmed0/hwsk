The most common type of hemophilia is called hemophilia A. This means the person does not have enough clotting factor VIII (factor eight). ... This person does not have enough clotting factor IX (factor nine). The result is the same for people with hemophilia A and B: they both bleed for a longer time than normal. People with hemophilia bleed easily, and the blood takes a longer time to clot. People with hemophilia can experience spontaneous or internal bleeding and often have painful, swollen joints due to bleeding into the joints. This rare but serious condition can have life-threatening complications. People with hemophilia also bleed, although they do not "gush" blood, as is commonly believed. They are not in any greater danger of bleeding to death than anyone else.
